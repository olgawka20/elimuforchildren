Tanzania: children and adolescents do not gain a secondary education

According to the Human Rights Watch, a total of 5.1 million children aged 7 to 17 are out of school, including nearly 1.5 million of lower secondary school age. A vast majority of children end their education after primary school.  Only 52 percent of the eligible school population, are enrolled in lower-secondary education (and not all of them complete it).  Challenges include child labour to help families meet their ends, child marriage (2 in 5 girls marry before the age of 18), lack of sources to finance school expenses. 


Education in United Nations' Sustainable Development Goals

The Sustainable Development Goals are a set of goals to be accompished by 2030, set by the UN to achieve a better and more sustainable future for all.  Education plays the key component of the goals, for example, Universal primary and secondary education: By 2030, ensure that all girls and boys complete free, equitable and quality primary and secondary education leading to relevant and effective learning outcomes. This means that all member states of the UN should provision of 12 years of free, publicly-funded, inclusive, equitable, quality primary and secondary education – of which at least nine years are compulsory, leading to relevant learning outcomes – should be ensured for all, without discrimination.  Project Elimu and Lescota Foundation are fully aligned with these goals and try to address the challenges in lack of access to education worldwide.
